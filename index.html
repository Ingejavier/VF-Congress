Renewable power agreements
How is Vodafone achieving its net-zero goal through Power Purchase Agreements.

  • PPAs help Vodafone support the development of renewable energy generation in the markets where we operate.

  • In Europe, 100% of the grid electricity Vodafone uses is provided via renewable tariffs, PPA or matched with renewable energy certificates.

  • We are working to achieve the same in our African markets by 2025.

  • By 2030, we aim to procure more than 30% of our total renewable electricity consumption through PPA contracts.

    • Pioneering renewable energy onsite
    Vodafone strives to deploy renewable technologies to reduce either energy taken from the grid or diesel fuels from our off grid sites.

    Offices & technical buildings
    We look to install rooftop solar PV on the sites we currently own.

    Base Stations & Mobile Access sites
    We are working to install solar PV and convert our diesel generators to battery and or solar hybrids to reduce associated emission, by reducing the generator run time by half.
    We are also trialling different wind technologies to further extend our renewable generation.
    Reducing energy consumption in Data Centres
    Vodafone is optimising the energy consumption in its chain Data Centres.

    More than one third of the electricity consumption in Vodafone Data Centres is due to the cooling systems to maintain the right temperature and humidity for our network elements.

    We have installed Machine Learning Cooling Controls in 75 sites across Europe with tens of thousands of geo-localized sensors measuring temperature and humidity.

    This information is processed by an AI algorithm to optimise the cooling supply with a reduction of electricity consumption and improving reliability
    Reducing Supply Chain carbon footprint
    Vodafone is reducing its emissions in its supply chain by:

    1. 1. Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP) - Deployed for over a decade which standardises and drives Vodafone’s suppliers to set targets, reduce emissions and adopt renewable energy.

    2. 2. ESG Criteria - In contractual commitments, 20% of the decision-making criteria is based on the ESG criteria, with 5% specifically on planet and emissions reduction commitments.

    3. 3. Educational Workshops - With our key suppliers to find hotspots to identify design, manufacturing and greater recycled material use opportunities to drive a ‘circular economy’.

    4. 4 Zero Waste - Driving zero waste with our target of 0% network waste by 2025 through creating and scaling marketplaces to drive greater reuse.
    Forest Fire Detection
    IoT sensors designed to detect forest fires during early stages.

    The IoT sensors monitor the microclimate, measuring temperature, humidity, and air pressure and they can run maintenance-free for 10-15 years without the need for batteries.

    This IoT ultra-fast fire detection solution connects the sensors to a cloud-based alert centre with Vodafone IoT connectivity, a much faster-acting way for wildfire detection.
    Leveraging Water Resources
    IoT Water Monitoring.

    Remote control of water flows

    Understanding how the flow of water is changing today is essential to optimise water use in the future as well as prevent the potential catastrophe, like water flows.

    Vodafone Business in collaboration with Oysta has developed special IoT sensors that allows administrations to monitor levels of flows remotely.

    Using Vodafone NB-IoT connectivity coverage, these long-lasting battery IoT sensors control reservoirs, sewers, wells, riverbeds and more; analysing data in real time and sending alerts and warnings.

    Open RAN
    Vodafone is building Europe’s first commercial Open RAN network in collaborations with its strategic vendors.
    Open RAN will lead to a more cost-effective, secure, energy efficient and customer-focused network of the future.

  • Energy Efficiency - Open RAN allows for the deployment of energy-efficient network equipment that consumes less power and reduces carbon emissions.

  • Reduced E-Waste - Open RAN promotes the use of standardized, interoperable network equipment, which reduces the need for proprietary hardware and software.

  • Bridging the Digital Divide - Open RAN can help extend wireless coverage to remote and underserved areas, promoting digital inclusion, providing access to essential services, and reducing inequalities.

  • Innovation - Open RAN enables new vendors to enter in the market offering new network solutions with new and more sustainable technologies and business models.

  • Disaster Response - Open RAN can facilitate the deployment of rapidly deployable wireless networks in emergency situations where communication infrastructure may be damaged or destroyed.

    Electric Fleet
    Vodafone is committed to transition its European fleet into fully electric by the end of 2028.

  • In order to reduce emissions, we are phasing our internal combustion vehicles by ensuring that from April 2024 only fully EVs or equivalent will be ordered by the company in Europe.


  • In FY22/23, 54% of Vodafone’s European fleet was fully electric or hybrid and we established the Vodafone Green Fleet Steering group to facilitate a transition to an electric fleet across all markets.


  • Vodafone Business Fleet Analytics keeps fleet management life simple, with real-time data supporting business decisions – all to protect teams, control costs & maximise efficiency, & reduce carbon emissions.
